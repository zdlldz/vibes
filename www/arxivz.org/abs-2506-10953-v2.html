Title: VIBE: A Novel Quantitative Framework for the Measurement of Vibrational-Intimate-Bureaucratic-Entropic Phenomena

Authors: Anita Goodvibe, Cal I. Bration, Ennui DuPont, Rex Onance, B.R. Ocratic

View PDF | HTML (experimental)

Abstract

We introduce the VIBE index, a composite scalar measure of the Vibrational-Intimate-Bureaucratic-Entropic state of a social environment, and describe the VIBEMETER Mark II field instrument for its measurement. Across 270 observations spanning nine social contexts in four cities, VIBE ranged from 0.110 to 0.921, with context means from 0.178 (government waiting room) to 0.866 (rave, 3:00am). The funeral reception mean was 0.534, and a cluster of elevated Intimacy observations in the waiting-room context suggests a Bureaucracy-Induced Intimacy Surge (BIIS). These results establish a rigorous empirical basis for vibe quantification.
