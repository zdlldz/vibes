VSC-0001:2026 (Excerpt): VIBE Index Definitions and Reference Framework

This publication establishes baseline terms, formula constraints, measurement conditions, and conformance obligations for certified VIBE reporting units.

Applied standards feed: implementation outcomes and committee sessions are published at VIBE-CON.

View conference program

Key to verbal forms: SHALL indicates a mandatory requirement, SHOULD indicates a recommendation, and MAY indicates a permitted option. Interpretation follows VSC/TR-001 conventions.

Foreword and Scope

This second edition cancels and replaces VSC-0001:2023 and formalizes the current framework for measuring Vibrational-Intimate-Bureaucratic-Entropic states.

VSC-0001:2026 governs the legal and technical interpretation of VIBE across instruments, field operators, policy reporting desks, and public communication contexts.

  • Normative domain: V, I, B, E scalar terms and compositing constraints.
  • Application domain: controlled study, public dashboards, and standards-compliant media reporting.
  • Authority status: mandatory for VSC certification pathways.

Note: instrument performance specifics are defined in VSC-0002 and field protocol specifics in VSC-0003.

Note: cross-species applications are under active committee development and are not normatively covered in this edition.

3. Composite Scoring and Anti-VIBE Boundary

Field approximation: VIBE = (V x I x B x E)1/4

Analytical form: VIBE = Z-1 · exp(-beta · HVIBE)

Interpretation note: Bureaucracy remains a normative inversion term (higher B indicates lower institutional drag).

Boundary note: candidate anti-VIBE observations (sub-index < 0) are flagged for VSC-0005 analysis and excluded from base conformance reporting in this edition.

4. Terms and Definitions (Snapshot)

  • VIBE index: composite scalar of social affective-thermodynamic state.
  • Null VIBE state: VIBE = 0 when any sub-index reaches minimum.
  • Bureaucracy-Induced Intimacy Surge (BIIS): transient intimacy lift in high-bureaucracy environments.
  • Social temperature (Tsoc): intensive parameter used in full analytical expression.
  • Ennui Quotient (EQ): normalized self-report instrument integrated into Entropy.

Annex B (Normative): Bureaucracy Inversion Rationale

B is defined as an inverse regulatory burden coefficient by design.

High B indicates lower institutional drag and higher vibe potential. Reversing this direction is a reporting non-conformance.
