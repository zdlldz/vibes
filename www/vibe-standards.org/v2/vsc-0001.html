Foreword

The VIBE Standards Council (VSC) is the international body responsible for the development, maintenance, and publication of standards governing the measurement, reporting, and certification of the Vibrational-Intimate-Bureaucratic-Entropic (VIBE) index. VSC operates under the principle that rigorous standardisation is a prerequisite for reproducible science, and that vibes — however colloquially employed — are no exception to this principle.

This second edition of VSC-0001 cancels and replaces the first edition (VSC-0001:2023), which has been technically revised. Principal changes from the first edition are as follows:

  • Clause 3: addition of terms 3.9 (social temperature), 3.10 (VIBE Hamiltonian), and 3.11 (anti-VIBE state);
  • Clause 4.4: revised definition of the Entropy sub-index (E) to incorporate the Ennui Quotient™ (EQ) instrument, now specified in full in Annex C;
  • Clause 5: complete analytical expression added as normative text; geometric mean approximation retained as field-use shorthand;
  • Annex A: updated reference scores to reflect Goodvibe et al. (2026).

Attention is drawn to the possibility that some elements of this document may be subject to intellectual property rights. VSC shall not be held responsible for identifying any or all such rights. In particular, the X-Factor Receptor™ (XFR) channel specification referenced in Clause 4.1 remains under separate cover pending resolution of patent application XFR-2024-EU-00441, and is not reproduced herein.

Approved by VSC/TC-1, 1 March 2026.

1 Scope

This document specifies:

  1. the definition of the Vibrational-Intimate-Bureaucratic-Entropic (VIBE) index as a composite scalar measure of the affective-thermodynamic state of a social environment;
  2. the four sub-indices comprising the VIBE index — Vibrations (V), Intimacy (I), Bureaucracy (B), and Entropy (E) — and their respective measurement domains;
  3. the units of measurement, normalisation conventions, and range constraints applicable to each sub-index and to the composite VIBE score;
  4. the reference formula for computing the composite VIBE score from sub-index values, including both the field approximation and the complete analytical expression;
  5. the colloquial reference anchors against which VIBE scores SHALL be calibrated for purposes of public reporting;
  6. minimum conditions for measurement validity;
  7. data reporting requirements for conformant VIBE measurements.

NOTE 1 — Instrument performance requirements are outside the scope of this document. See VSC-0002 (VIBEMETER: Performance Requirements and Calibration Protocol).

NOTE 2 — This document applies to VIBE measurements conducted in human social contexts. Cross-species applications are under development by VSC/TC-1/WG5 and are not addressed herein. Whether non-human animals generate or perceive vibes remains an open empirical question (see Goodvibe et al., 2026, §4).

3 Terms and Definitions

For the purposes of this document, the following terms and definitions apply.

3.1 vibe
The composite affective-thermodynamic state of a social environment at a given moment in time, as quantified by the VIBE index (3.4). Colloquially: "the vibe."

NOTE — The noun "vibe" is distinct from the verb "to vibe," which is informal, not standardised, and outside the scope of this document.

3.2 social environment
Any bounded space, situation, or temporal context in which one or more persons are co-present, whether physically or mediated by technology.
3.3 sub-index
Any one of the four component measures — Vibrations (V), Intimacy (I), Bureaucracy (B), or Entropy (E) — whose values, in combination, determine the composite VIBE score.
3.4 VIBE index
The composite scalar quantity, denoted VIBE, representing the aggregate affective-thermodynamic state of a social environment. Computed as specified in Clause 5. Range: [0, 1] under standard conditions; see 3.11 for anti-VIBE extension.

NOTE — A VIBE score of 0.0 does not indicate the absence of measurement; it indicates that one or more sub-indices has reached its minimum value, thereby suppressing the composite. See the null VIBE state (3.6).

3.5 VIBEMETER
A conformant field instrument for the simultaneous measurement of all four VIBE sub-indices. Conformance requirements are specified in VSC-0002.
3.6 null VIBE state
The condition in which VIBE = 0, arising when any sub-index equals zero. A consequence of the multiplicative composite structure (see Clause 5.1). Colloquially equivalent to: "the vibes were off."
3.7 maximum VIBE state
The condition in which VIBE = 1.0, requiring all four sub-indices to simultaneously reach their calibration maxima. Colloquially equivalent to: "it's a vibe."

NOTE — The maximum VIBE state has not been observed in field conditions. The highest value recorded in the available empirical literature is 0.921 (Goodvibe et al., 2026).

3.8 Bureaucracy-Induced Intimacy Surge (BIIS)
An empirically observed transient elevation of the Intimacy sub-index (I) in high-Bureaucracy environments (B < 0.15), attributed to interpersonal bonds formed through shared adversity. First documented in Goodvibe et al. (2026), government waiting room context. Not yet normatively standardised; see VSC-0005 (draft).
3.9 social temperature (Tsoc)
A novel intensive thermodynamic quantity representing the mean kinetic energy of interpersonal exchange per unit social mass within a given social environment. Unit: J·kg⁻¹·soc⁻¹. Used in the complete analytical expression for the VIBE index (see Clause 5.2).

NOTE — Tsoc is a derived quantity, not directly measurable by a VIBEMETER. Its determination under field conditions is specified in VSC-0003:2024, Annex C (normative). Standard social conditions are defined as Tsoc ∈ [0.4, 0.8] on the normalised social temperature scale.

3.10 VIBE Hamiltonian (HVIBE)
The operator expression used in the complete analytical derivation of the composite VIBE score. Defined as HVIBE = −(1/4)Σi ln(φi) over the four sub-indices φ ∈ {V, I, B, E}. See Clause 5.2.
3.11 anti-VIBE state
A condition in which one or more sub-indices takes a negative value, indicating active suppression of the corresponding dimension rather than mere absence. Anti-VIBE states produce composite VIBE scores below zero. They are outside the scope of the current edition of this standard; see VSC-0005:2026 (draft), Anti-VIBE States: Extended Measurement Framework. Colloquially equivalent to: "bad vibes."
3.12 inverse regulatory burden coefficient
The operationalisation of the Bureaucracy sub-index (B), such that B = 1 represents complete freedom from institutional constraint and B = 0 represents total bureaucratic saturation. The direction of this operationalisation is normative. See Clause 4.3 and Annex B.
3.13 Ennui Quotient™ (EQ)
A single-item self-report instrument administered to co-present subjects during a VIBE measurement session as part of the Entropy sub-index determination. Full specification in Annex C.

NOTE — The Ennui Quotient™ is a registered instrument of the VSC. The question text ("How bleh do you feel right now?") is normative and SHALL NOT be paraphrased, reworded, or replaced with a synonym for "bleh" without prior written authorisation from VSC/TC-1.

4 VIBE Index Components

The VIBE index is composed of four sub-indices, each normalised to the closed interval [0, 1] under standard conditions. Each sub-index SHALL be determined using a conformant VIBEMETER (VSC-0002) unless an exemption is granted under the provisions of VSC-0003:2024, Clause 6.4.

4.1 Vibrations (V)

The Vibrations sub-index measures the total vibrational energy density of the social environment, integrating contributions from the following sensor channels of the VIBEMETER:

  • acoustic array (20 Hz–20 kHz; infrasound extension to 0.1 Hz required);
  • photon flux detector (luminance variation sensitivity ≥ 0.5 lux·s⁻¹);
  • MEMS anemometer (atmospheric kinetic energy; air velocity resolution ≤ 0.1 m·s⁻¹);
  • micro-seismic accelerometer (3-axis; sensitivity ≥ 10⁻⁶ g);
  • X-Factor Receptor™ (XFR) channel.

V = 0 denotes total energetic quiescence. V = 1 denotes maximum vibrational richness as defined by the VIBEMETER calibration reference (see Clause 7.2).

NOTE 1 — The XFR channel captures residual vibrational signal not accounted for by the acoustic, photonic, atmospheric, or micro-seismic channels. Its specification is maintained under separate instrument cover (Ref: XFR-2024-EU-00441) pending resolution of applicable intellectual property restrictions. Operators SHALL use a conformant VIBEMETER to ensure correct XFR integration. Independent reproduction of XFR functionality is a non-conformance.

NOTE 2 — The precise nature of the X-Factor captured by the XFR channel is a matter of ongoing scientific inquiry. The VSC takes no position on this question. The channel works. That is sufficient for purposes of standardisation.

4.2 Intimacy (I)

The Intimacy sub-index measures the aggregate interpersonal and neurophysiological proximity coefficient of co-present subjects, integrating outputs from the following sensor channels of the VIBEMETER:

  • infrared thermometer (dual-band; range 20–42°C; body heat gradient between subjects);
  • BrainSync™ optical probe (fNIRS-adjacent; neurological coherence index output);
  • galvanic skin response array (perspiration index; resolution ≤ 0.05 μS);
  • respiratory coherence monitor (coupling coefficient output required);
  • Semantic Proximity Analyzer™ (SPA) (ratio output: expressed speech to demonstrably withheld speech).

I = 0 denotes complete social isolation (or the absence of co-present subjects). I = 1 denotes maximum interpersonal coherence as defined by the VIBEMETER calibration reference.

NOTE 1 — The SPA channel quantifies the ratio of expressed speech to demonstrably withheld speech — that is, words said relative to words unsaid — via contextual gap analysis of ambient discourse. Operators SHALL NOT prompt subjects in any way that alters the natural ratio of expressed to withheld speech during a measurement session. The VSC considers deliberate elicitation of withheld speech during measurement to be an interference condition and will not certify results obtained under such conditions.

NOTE 2 — Environments in which n = 1 person is present represent a boundary condition for the Intimacy sub-index. Such environments are not excluded from measurement; however, I values obtained at n = 1 SHALL be flagged in the measurement record. Existing data suggests that vibes may be technically generated but not received under such conditions (Goodvibe et al., 2026, §3).

4.3 Bureaucracy (B)

The Bureaucracy sub-index is operationalised as the inverse regulatory burden coefficient. This operationalisation is normative:

  • B = 1 represents the complete absence of institutional constraint within the social environment.
  • B = 0 represents total bureaucratic saturation.

B is determined from the following VIBEMETER sensor channels:

  • Regulatory Burden Index (RBI) scanner (jurisdiction database updated ≥ annually);
  • Paperwork Density Gauge (PDG) (optical document counter; sheet-equivalent normalisation required);
  • Authority Proximity Sensor (APS) (institutional rank weighting table updated ≥ annually).

NOTE — The inversion in this clause is a deliberate and load-bearing design decision. High B scores indicate high freedom from institutional constraint, and therefore high vibe potential in the Bureaucracy dimension. Low B scores indicate heavy regulatory burden and correspondingly suppressed vibe potential. Presenting VIBE data without noting this inversion, or representing B as a direct measure of bureaucratic density, is a non-conformance under VSC-0004. See Annex B for technical rationale.

4.4 Entropy (E)

The Entropy sub-index measures the entropic richness of the social environment — the availability and complexity of affective-informational states accessible to co-present subjects. Following Shannon (1948), Boltzmann (1877), and Clausius (1865), E is operationalised as the normalised information entropy of the composite ambient signal as measured by the VIBEMETER, integrated with the output of the Ennui Quotient™ (EQ) instrument (see Annex C).

E = 0 denotes a settled, affectively depleted environment. E = 1 denotes maximum emotional and informational complexity as defined by the VIBEMETER calibration reference.

NOTE 1 — E is a measure of affective complexity, not of disorder in the pejorative sense. A high-E environment is not a chaotic environment; it is a rich one. The VSC acknowledges that this distinction is philosophically contested, and refers enquirers to the referenced literature.

NOTE 2 — In environments where subject self-report is not practicable (e.g., observation of a sleeping population; measurement at institutions where direct subject interaction is prohibited by their own regulations), operators MAY omit the EQ channel. This omission SHALL be noted in the measurement record under VSC-0004 and the resulting E value flagged accordingly. Omission of the EQ channel without notation is a non-conformance.

NOTE 3 — The colloquial concept of ennui — a state of high personal entropy combined with low ambient social entropy — is captured specifically by the EQ instrument. The VSC notes that ennui is both a valid affective state and an underappreciated thermodynamic phenomenon, and declines to editorialize further.

5 Composite Scoring

5.1 Field Approximation

For standard field use, the composite VIBE score SHALL be computed as the geometric mean of the four sub-indices:

VIBE = (V × I × B × E)1/4

This formulation ensures that any sub-index value of zero produces a composite score of zero, consistent with the null VIBE state (3.6). The composite VIBE score admits no partial credit across dimensions: a sufficiently bureaucratic environment will suppress the composite index regardless of vibrational or intimate conditions. This is not a limitation of the model. It reflects how vibes work.

5.2 Complete Analytical Expression

The complete analytical expression for the VIBE index, derived from the Gibbs-Boltzmann statistical mechanical framework, is:

VIBE = Z−1 · exp(−β · HVIBE)

where:

  • HVIBE = −(1/4)Σi ln(φi) is the VIBE Hamiltonian (3.10), summed over the four sub-indices φ ∈ {V, I, B, E};
  • β = (kB · Tsoc)−1 is the inverse social temperature (3.9), with kB the Boltzmann constant and Tsoc the social temperature of the environment;
  • Z is the VIBE partition function, normalising the expression across all accessible microstates of the social system.

NOTE 1 — Under standard social conditions (see 3.9, Note), the complete analytical expression reduces to the geometric mean given in Clause 5.1. The two formulations are therefore equivalent for all practical field applications.

NOTE 2 — The partition function Z is not directly observable. Its value is determined by the VSC Reference Social Condition library maintained under VSC-0003. Operators are not required to compute Z independently. A conformant VIBEMETER performs this computation internally.

NOTE 3 — The complete analytical expression is provided as normative text because precision is a value the VSC holds unconditionally, even when — especially when — the field approximation produces an identical result.

5.3 Anti-VIBE States

In a small proportion of field observations, sub-index values have been observed to approach or cross zero in the negative direction, yielding composite VIBE scores below zero. These are designated anti-VIBE states (3.11).

Anti-VIBE states are outside the scope of the current edition of this standard. Observations in which any sub-index takes a negative value SHALL be excluded from analyses conducted under this standard and flagged in the measurement record as "anti-VIBE candidate." Such observations SHOULD be retained and submitted to the VSC Reference Dataset for use in developing VSC-0005.

NOTE — Four anti-VIBE candidate observations were recorded in the foundational empirical dataset (Goodvibe et al., 2026). Their contexts have not been disclosed in the published literature, consistent with VSC data ethics policy. The VSC considers this appropriate.

7 Measurement Conditions

7.1 Instrument Requirements

VIBE measurements intended for publication, regulatory reporting, commercial application, or conformant data submission SHALL be conducted using a VIBEMETER that has been certified under VSC-0002. Use of non-certified instruments is permitted for exploratory or personal purposes but SHOULD be disclosed, and results obtained with non-certified instruments SHALL NOT be represented as VSC-conformant.

NOTE — Consumer-grade VIBE estimator tools (e.g., questionnaire-based instruments, mobile applications) are not VIBEMETER substitutes and do not produce conformant results. The VSC does not endorse any consumer estimator product, though it acknowledges their value as gateway experiences for the general public.

7.2 Calibration

Prior to each measurement session, the VIBEMETER SHALL be calibrated against the VSC Reference Social Environment Type 3 (RSE-3), or against an alternative reference condition approved by VSC/TC-1 under the provisions of VSC-0002, Clause 7.

RSE-3 is defined as follows: a standardised configuration consisting of seven (7) to nine (9) persons gathered in a domestic kitchen environment on a weekend afternoon, with at least one window open, ambient music at conversational volume or below, and no institutional paperwork present. RSE-3 has a target composite VIBE of 0.72 ± 0.04 under standard social conditions.

NOTE 1 — The requirement that no institutional paperwork be present during calibration is normative. The VSC has observed that even the visual proximity of unprocessed administrative documents measurably depresses the Bureaucracy sub-index of the calibration environment, introducing systematic downward bias into subsequent field measurements. Operators SHOULD remove, conceal, or destroy any such documents before initiating calibration.

NOTE 2 — Operators MAY petition VSC/TC-1 for approval of alternative reference social environments. Applications must be submitted on Form VSC-CAL-01 with supporting measurement data from a minimum of five independent RSE-3 cross-calibrations. Processing time: 90–180 days. The VSC apologises for nothing.

7.3 Operator Qualification

Measurements submitted to the VSC Reference Dataset or cited in conformant publications SHALL be conducted by operators holding current accreditation under the VSC Operator Accreditation Scheme (OAS). Accreditation requirements are specified in VSC-0003, Clause 8.

Operators SHALL be blinded to expected VIBE scores for the social environments under measurement. Knowledge of expected scores prior to data collection is an invalidating condition.

7.4 Observation Duration

Each VIBE observation SHALL consist of a minimum deployment duration of fifteen (15) minutes. Sub-index values SHALL be averaged over the full observation window. Observations of less than fifteen minutes SHALL be flagged as "short-duration" and SHOULD NOT be used in summary statistics without explicit disclosure.

NOTE — The fifteen-minute minimum reflects the empirically determined minimum required for VIBE stabilisation following instrument deployment. Social environments respond to the presence of a VIBEMETER. Whether this constitutes observer effect in the quantum sense is outside the scope of this standard.

8.3 Prohibited Representations

The following representations are prohibited in conformant reporting:

  1. Presenting B as a direct measure of bureaucratic density (rather than its inverse). See Clause 4.3 and Annex B.
  2. Reporting a VIBE score without the corresponding sub-index values in any context where the sub-index breakdown is material to interpretation.
  3. Representing a VIBE score obtained with a non-certified instrument as VSC-conformant.
  4. Representing a short-duration observation (< 15 minutes) as a full conformant observation without flagging.
  5. Asserting that a VIBE score of 1.0 has been observed without submission of supporting measurement data to VSC/TC-1 for independent verification. The VSC treats claims of maximum VIBE with appropriate scientific scepticism.

9 Conformance

A measurement, system, instrument, or data record claims conformance with this standard if and only if it satisfies all normative requirements in Clauses 1 through 8 and Annexes B and C.

Conformance is self-declaratory for individual measurement records. Instrument conformance requires certification under VSC-0002. Operator conformance requires accreditation under VSC-0003, Clause 8.

Partial conformance — in which a system satisfies some but not all normative requirements — is not recognised by the VSC. A system either conforms or it does not. This mirrors the behaviour of the VIBE index itself under the geometric mean formulation.

NOTE — Questions regarding conformance assessment MAY be directed to VSC/TC-1 via the VSC secretariat. The VSC does not provide informal conformance opinions by electronic mail, verbal communication, or interpretive dance.

Annex B (Normative) Technical Rationale for B-Inversion

This Annex explains and affirms the normative decision to operationalise the Bureaucracy sub-index (B) as the inverse regulatory burden coefficient.

B.1 Background. The relationship between bureaucratic constraint and vibe expression is empirically well-established. Frequencies & Wellbeing (2019) demonstrated that bureaucratic friction measurably suppresses ambient social energy across a range of institutional contexts. The government waiting room context in Goodvibe et al. (2026) produced the lowest composite VIBE score observed in the foundational dataset (0.178), driven by a mean B of 0.083 — the lowest value recorded. The finding is unambiguous: bureaucracy, acting alone, is sufficient to destroy a vibe.

B.2 Design decision. To ensure that a high B score reflects a high contribution to composite VIBE — consistent with the geometric mean formulation in Clause 5.1 — B is defined as the inverse regulatory burden coefficient. B = 1 means no bureaucracy. B = 0 means total saturation. This inversion is non-negotiable and is not subject to revision by operator preference, institutional convention, or intuition.

B.3 Common misapplication. The most frequent non-conformance identified by VSC auditors is the representation of B as a direct measure of bureaucratic density. This error produces inverted conclusions: the government waiting room would appear to score highest on B, and the rave lowest. This is wrong. The VSC has made the direction of this sub-index as clear as it knows how to make a direction, and will not be held responsible for any further confusion.

B.4 Paradoxical exception: BIIS. The anomalous Bureaucracy-Induced Intimacy Surge observations (3.8) suggest that under extreme bureaucratic conditions (B < 0.15), the Intimacy sub-index (I) may transiently elevate due to social bonding through shared adversity. This effect does not alter the definition or direction of B. It is a finding about I. The VSC notes this distinction is important.

Annex C (Normative) The Ennui Quotient™ Instrument

C.1 Purpose. The Ennui Quotient™ (EQ) is a single-item self-report instrument that captures the ennui dimension of the Entropy (E) sub-index. It is administered to co-present subjects during a VIBE measurement session and its output is integrated with the VIBEMETER's ambient entropy estimate to produce the composite E value.

C.2 Item text. The EQ instrument consists of the following item, administered verbally or in writing to each co-present subject:

"How bleh do you feel right now?"

1 — Not at all 2 3 4 5 — Profoundly

The item text is normative. It SHALL NOT be paraphrased, reworded, translated into synonyms, or accompanied by explanatory framing that might alter the subject's response. The word "bleh" is intentional. It has been validated across fourteen linguistic contexts. Substituting a clinical or technical synonym (e.g., "listless," "dysthymic," "affectively flat") alters the construct being measured and constitutes a non-conformance.

C.3 Scoring. Raw EQ responses SHALL be standardised using the population norms maintained by VSC/TC-1 before integration into the E sub-index. Operators SHALL NOT use raw EQ scores as direct E values.

C.4 Administration conditions. The EQ SHALL be administered within the final five minutes of the observation window, after the VIBEMETER has reached measurement stability. Administering the EQ at the start of a session risks the Heisenberg effect: the act of asking subjects how bleh they feel may itself alter how bleh they feel. The VSC considers this to be an unacceptable source of variance and has timed the administration window accordingly.

NOTE — In the event that a subject responds to the EQ with a question rather than a rating (e.g., "What does bleh mean?"), the operator SHALL respond: "Whatever it means to you." No further guidance is normatively provided. This is, in the VSC's view, the correct answer.
